Frequently Asked Questions

How do solar systems work?

Solar panels are made up of photovoltaic (PV) cells made of silicon. When the sun’s rays hit them, these cells convert sunlight to electricity. Individual cells are wired together to form a solar panel. Panels are typically three feet by five feet. They are coated in tempered glass, which allows them to withstand harsh weather.

How do I get electric energy at night or on cloudy days?

When Solar energy is generated during the day, if stored properly using storage devices like a battery, it is possible to have electric energy for home use at night. Solar electricity works when Sun’s light is available because it is this sun’s photon that strikes the Solar panels that are converted to electricity for home and business use. During cloudy days, minimal energy can be stored since solar needs the Sun and at night it is only the capacity of the energy stored in the battery that the inverter converts for use.

How long does a solar-installation last?

Think of your solar array as a 25-year investment. Solar panels will produce electricity for at least 25 years. Panels will continue to generate electricity after 25 years, but at a decreasing rate. While microinverters will likely last for the duration of the PV system, you may need to replace central inverters after 15 years.
